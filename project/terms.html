Terminology

Ball-In-Hand
When one player fouls in a game, via scratching or hitting the wrong ball (or any other non-8 ball table foul), the other player may take the cue ball and place it anywhere on the table. This applies to any fouls made during the game, except for those made during the break or made on the 8-ball.
Bank
When a player hits the cue ball into an object ball and the object ball goes into a rail before going into a pocket, the player is "banking"
Break and Run
When a player breaks and makes something in, followed by running out the rest of their balls, it is referred to as a "break and run."
Draw
When a player hits below center on the cue ball, they apply "draw," or backwards spin.
English
"English" is another term for applying any kind of spin to the cue ball.
Follow
When a player hits above center on the cue ball, the player is applying forward spin, or "follow."
"In the kitchen"
The space behind the second diamond from the breaking end of the table. Better known as the area from which you are supposed to break or use ball-in-hand after the break.
Kick
When a player shoots the cue ball into a rail before the cue ball comes into contact with an object ball, they are "kicking" at the object ball.
Miscue
When a player strokes at the cue ball, but something causes them to not properly hit the cue ball. This is usually caused by not chalking their cue enough, poor form in their stroke, and attempting to hit too low or too high on the cue ball.
Pocket Speed
The exact amount of speed required to get an object into a pocket.
Run
When a player pockets more than 2 balls in one turn, it is called a "run." Thus, a "run-out" is when a player pockets all of their remaining balls in one turn.
Safety
When a player makes a shot with no intention of pocketing a ball, but with intending to leave the other player and difficult shot.
